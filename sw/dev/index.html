🧥 What to Expect

Typical October temperatures are around 77°F daytime and 55°F overnight. Autumn colors begin to emerge, with trees displaying shades of red and gold against the desert backdrop. Crowds are lighter than summer, making for a more relaxed experience. Most attractions are open, but some trails may have seasonal conditions to be aware of.

Take I-15 S to UT-9 E for a direct route into St. George. The drive features scenic views of the Virgin River as you approach the city. Ample parking is available in downtown St. George.

🏔️ Top Attractions

🏛️Zion National Park
Must-Seefull day
Zion features towering cliffs and diverse ecosystems. The scenic drive offers numerous pull-offs and viewpoints, including the iconic Court of the Patriarchs. Be aware that entry requires a timed reservation during peak hours.📌 Timed entry is required for access during peak hours.
🏛️Snow Canyon State Park
Moderate2–3 hrs
Snow Canyon features unique red rock formations and lava tubes. The park offers several trails, including the easy Lava Flow Trail and the more challenging Johnson Canyon Trail. Expect to see desert wildlife and striking geological features.📌 No shuttle service; a vehicle is required for access.
📍Red Cliffs Desert Reserve
Easy1 hr
This reserve features striking red rock formations and a network of trails. The area is ideal for a short hike or picnic with scenic views of the surrounding desert landscape. Birdwatching is also popular here.
📍Canyon View Park
1 hr
This park features expansive views of the surrounding landscape, including St. George and the distant mountains. It’s a popular spot for picnics and leisurely walks.
🏛️St. George Dinosaur Discovery Site
1–2 hrs
This site showcases well-preserved dinosaur tracks and fossils in a natural setting. Interactive displays provide insight into the region's prehistoric past. The site is family-friendly and offers guided tours.📌 Check for tour availability upon arrival.
🏛️Tuacahn Amphitheatre
2–3 hrs
Tuacahn hosts live theatrical performances in a stunning outdoor setting against the red cliffs. Check the schedule in advance for current shows. The amphitheater offers a unique experience combining nature and the arts.📌 Tickets should be purchased in advance due to limited seating.
🚗Zion Canyon Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive6 miles one-way
Zion Canyon Scenic Drive follows the Virgin River through towering red cliffs and unique rock formations. Key stops include the Court of the Patriarchs at mile 1.5 and Zion Lodge at mile 2.5. This drive requires a shuttle during peak months, but October sees fewer crowds, allowing for easier access and parking. The fall colors add warmth to the landscape, enhancing the views.
🚗Kolob Terrace Road
Day Trip20 miles one-way
Kolob Terrace Road ascends through pine forests and offers expansive views of the surrounding canyons and plateaus. Key viewpoints include the Lava Point Overlook at mile 11, which provides a panoramic vista of Zion National Park. The gravel road is well-maintained but may require a high-clearance vehicle in certain sections. October's clear skies and fall foliage make the drive particularly rewarding.
🚗Red Cliffs Desert Reserve
Viewpoint15-min drive from St. George
Red Cliffs Desert Reserve features striking red rock formations and unique desert landscapes. The viewpoint offers views of the prominent cliffs and the surrounding desert flora. Parking is available at the trailhead, but it can fill quickly on weekends. The clear October skies and cooler temperatures make this an ideal time for photography.
🚗Snow Canyon Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive5 miles one-way
Snow Canyon Scenic Drive winds through dramatic sandstone cliffs and lava flows, showcasing the area's geological diversity. Notable stops include the West Canyon Overlook at mile 2 and the Petrified Dunes at mile 3.5. The road is paved and accessible for all vehicles. October's mild temperatures and colorful foliage create a pleasant driving experience.

Zion National Park

October 18, 2026

NPS / Ally O'Rullian

What to Know About Zion National Park

Zion National Park is a remote destination with a focus on natural beauty and outdoor activities. In October, visitors can engage in park-led interpretive programs, including ranger talks and evening programs at the amphitheater. The nearest gateway town, Springdale, offers a relaxed atmosphere with local art galleries and occasional live music, but specific events during this time are limited.

NPS / Ally O'Rullian

🧥 What to Expect

Typical October temperatures are around 62°F daytime and 38°F overnight. October reveals the vibrant autumn colors of Zion's foliage, with red and gold leaves contrasting against the canyon walls. Fall crowds are lower than in summer, making for a more serene experience.

🏔️ Top Attractions

🥾Angels Landing
Must-SeeStrenuous4–5 hrs round-trip
Angels Landing features a steep ascent of 1,488 feet over 5.0 miles with narrow, exposed sections. The final stretch requires a chain-assisted scramble. A permit is required for day hikes, obtained via a lottery system.📌 Permit required for day hikes via lottery.
🥾The Narrows
Must-SeeModerate2–8 hrs, depending on how far you go
Hike through the Virgin River in a narrow canyon with towering walls. The hike can be adjusted for time and water conditions. Water shoes are essential for comfort.📌 Water shoes essential; check water levels before hiking.
🥾Emerald Pools Trail
Easy1–2 hrs round-trip
This trail leads to three beautiful pools with waterfalls and stunning views of the canyon. The hike is about 1.0 mile to the lower pool with minimal elevation gain.📌 Accessed via the park shuttle.
🥾Weeping Rock
Easy30 min
This short trail leads to an overhanging rock alcove with dripping springs and lush vegetation. It is a quick, accessible stop.📌 Trail may close due to rockfall hazards.
🏛️Kolob Canyons
1–2 hrs
This section of Zion features spectacular red cliffs and a scenic drive. The 5-mile scenic drive offers several viewpoints and trail access.📌 Access is via a separate entrance; check hours before visiting.
📍Zion Canyon Scenic Drive
1 hr + stops
This scenic drive showcases the grandeur of Zion Canyon with multiple pullouts for photos and viewpoints. It's best enjoyed with a few stops along the way.📌 Shuttle required during peak season.
🚗Zion Canyon Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive6 miles one-way
Zion Canyon Scenic Drive follows the Virgin River, flanked by towering sandstone cliffs. Key viewpoints include Court of the Patriarchs at mile 1.1 and Weeping Rock at mile 2.4. The drive is closed to private vehicles during peak season; use the park shuttle instead. In October, enjoy fall colors contrasting with the red rock, and the light is ideal for photography in the morning.
🚗Mount Carmel Scenic Highway
Day Trip24 miles one-way
Mount Carmel Scenic Highway offers expansive views of Zion's eastern cliffs and the surrounding landscape. Stops at the East Entrance and the Canyon Overlook provide sweeping vistas of the park's rugged terrain. The road is well-maintained and suitable for all vehicles. October features cooler weather, making for comfortable driving and clear visibility of distant peaks.

🧭 CAN'T-MISS ENROUTE

🏔️ Top Attractions

🥾Navajo Loop Trail
Must-SeeModerate1.5–2 hours round-trip
This popular trail descends into the canyon, featuring tight switchbacks and views of the towering hoodoos. The trail is approximately 1.3 miles long with an elevation change of about 500 feet. Best hiked in the morning for cooler temperatures and fewer crowds.📌 No permit required.
🥾Queens Garden Trail
Must-SeeModerate2–3 hours round-trip
A 1.8-mile trail that leads to the base of the canyon, showcasing some of the park's most famous hoodoos. The trail features gentle grades and stunning views of the formations along the way.📌 No permit required.
🥾Fairyland Loop
Strenuous4–5 hours round-trip
This 8-mile loop offers a less crowded experience and views of unique rock formations. Expect elevation changes and a more challenging terrain.📌 No permit required.
📍Bryce Point
30 min
This viewpoint offers expansive views of the Bryce Amphitheater and the surrounding landscape. It is a great spot for photography.📌 Parking available nearby.
🎯Dark Sky Stargazing
1–2 hours
Bryce Canyon is designated as a Dark Sky Park, making it ideal for stargazing. The clear October skies provide excellent visibility for celestial events.📌 Bring a blanket and flashlight for comfortable viewing.
📍Sunrise Point
30 min
A popular viewpoint for its panoramic views of the canyon. It’s especially beautiful at sunrise as the light enhances the colors of the hoodoos.📌 Easily accessible from the main park road.
🚗Bryce Canyon Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive18 miles one-way
Bryce Canyon Scenic Drive winds through a unique landscape of colorful hoodoos and natural amphitheaters. Key viewpoints include Sunrise Point and Sunset Point, both offering dramatic perspectives of the canyon's formations. The road has several pull-outs for photographs, and parking is available at each viewpoint. In October, the fall colors enhance the scenery, with fewer visitors making for a more tranquil experience.
🚗Fairyland Point
Viewpoint10-min drive from park entrance
Fairyland Point provides a sweeping view of the Bryce Canyon amphitheater, with notable formations like the Chinese Wall and the Castle. The viewpoint is easily accessible with a short drive and a brief walk from the parking area. Restrooms are available nearby, but parking can fill up quickly during peak hours. In October, the angle of the sun casts vibrant shadows on the hoodoos, enhancing the visual experience.
🚗Rim Road to Rainbow Point
Day Trip38 miles round-trip
Rim Road to Rainbow Point offers a scenic loop with multiple overlooks showcasing Bryce's unique geology and vast landscapes. Stop at Ponderosa Point and Yovimpa Point for expansive views of the canyon and distant mountains. The road is well-maintained, but it is advisable to check for any seasonal closures. October provides crisp air and vibrant fall foliage, making this drive particularly striking.

What to Know About Capitol Reef National Park

Capitol Reef National Park is a remote setting with a focus on natural beauty and outdoor activities rather than ticketed cultural events. In October, visitors can participate in interpretive programs such as ranger-led talks and evening programs at the amphitheater. The nearby town of Torrey offers a few local amenities, including occasional live music and community gatherings, but specific events may not be scheduled during this time.

Local tip: Check out the ranger-led programs at the visitor center for educational talks and activities during your visit. These programs are typically available daily and provide insight into the park's unique geology and ecology. More info

🏔️ Top Attractions

🥾Hickman Bridge
Must-SeeModerate1.5 hours round-trip
A 2-mile round-trip trail leads to a 133-ft natural bridge. The hike offers views of the surrounding cliffs and canyon. Best visited in the morning for cooler temperatures.📌 No permits required.
🏛️Fruita Historic District
Must-See1 hour
This historic area features a collection of early settler structures and an orchard. It's a great spot for a leisurely stroll, especially in the fall when the trees are colorful.📌 Visitor center available for guidance.
🥾Cassidy Arch
Moderate3 hours round-trip
A 3-mile round-trip hike leads to Cassidy Arch, which spans 90 ft. The trail climbs gradually, providing views of the surrounding landscape.📌 Best visited in the morning to avoid afternoon heat.
🥾Grand Wash Trail
Easy2–3 hours round-trip
This 4.4-mile round-trip trail takes you through a narrow wash flanked by towering cliffs. Look for unique rock formations along the way.📌 Best hiked in cooler parts of the day.
🏛️Fremont Petroglyphs
30 min
These ancient rock carvings are easily accessible and provide insight into the area's early inhabitants. A short, flat trail leads to the petroglyphs.📌 No permits required.
🎯Gifford Homestead
30 min
A historic farmhouse that serves as a bakery. Stop in for homemade pies and preserves, offering a taste of local history.📌 Cash only; open seasonally.
📍Goosenecks Overlook
30 min
This viewpoint offers a dramatic look at the meandering river below. It's a short walk from the parking area and ideal for photography.📌 Accessible via a short path from the parking area.
🚗Capitol Reef Scenic Drive
Scenic Drive8 miles one-way
Capitol Reef Scenic Drive winds through the park's unique geology, showcasing the Waterpocket Fold's colorful cliffs and rock formations. Key stops include the Goosenecks Overlook at mile 3.2 and the Gifford Homestead at mile 5, where you can purchase local fruit products. The drive is paved and accessible in any vehicle, but expect some gravel sections. October's cooler temperatures enhance the vibrant fall colors in the foliage along the route.
🚗Cohab Canyon Viewpoint
Viewpoint10-min drive from visitor center
Cohab Canyon Viewpoint provides a stunning overview of the canyon's rugged terrain and the surrounding cliffs. From this point, see iconic formations like Cassidy Arch and the towering red rock walls. The viewpoint has a small parking area, so arrive early to secure a spot. The October weather offers crisp air and fewer visitors, making for a peaceful experience at this scenic overlook.
